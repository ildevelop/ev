]]> Displayed on numerous websites across the world, these pay-per-click ads allow you to target customers who you believe are right for your product or service. The advantage is obvious. Rather than pay to post a generic ad, you instead pay only when somebody is interested enough to click your ad. This means the quality of your traffic skyrockets, and hopefully, if you’ve run your ad campaign properly, your profits, too.

Using Google AdWords PPC Advertising

It’s easy to get your AdWords account setup. All you need to do to start your Google PPC campaign is open an account with Google and deposit some funds. It’s really that easy.

Then, you create your ad and send it out for the world to see. You should start getting clicks fairly quickly. The scope of these Google ads is vast, so straight away your business will be thrust into the limelight.

Google PPC Cost

One of the trickiest aspects of Google PPC advertising is figuring out the cost-per-click and matching it up to your product conversion rate.

For example, if you pay $1 a click, but only one in ten clicks results in a sale, you’ll be paying $10 for every sale. If your profit margin is $30, that’s a good deal. If it’s only $12 though, you might want to tweak your campaign a little for better results.

Maximizing Efficiency

It’s unlikely your first Google AdWords campaign will be a roaring success. Most people find theirs moves along fairly nicely, but doesn’t quite have the efficiency they’d like.

At this point, they usually choose to change something. It can be a little nerve wracking changing a formula that isn’t necessarily broken, but sometimes this is for the best. As you’re paying for each click, you’ll want to achieve maximum efficiency when possible.

One of the first things that’s changed is the wording of the ad. Google PPC advertising responds almost in real time, so you’ll be able to see whether or not your change is successful. You can also run a new ad in unison to see which of the two performs better.

Understanding Google PPC Cost

Google PPC cost can differ so wildly it’s impossible to give you even a rough idea of what you could expect to pay per click. Luckily, the Google AdWords tool can tell you though. By searching for a keyword and its click-through value, you’ll be able to see which keywords are suitable for your budget

If Google AdWords PPC management sounds like a daunting task, you always have the option of outsourcing the job to the professionals. Those with experience of running such campaigns will be able to help you maximize your revenue while cutting down your advertising budget. As many people are unfamiliar with the little nuisances of a campaign, outsourcing is usually a popular choice

What are Conversion Rates?

Whether you’re a marketing novice or a seasoned pro, conversion rates are something you’ll be able to understand right off the bat. That’s because the formula for working them out is so simple.

Your ecommerce conversion rate, for example, is worked out by dividing the number of sales you made by the number of visitors to your site, then multiplying that figure by a hundred. If 100 people viewed your site today but only 4 people bought something from you, your conversion rate would be 4%.

The higher your conversion rate, the better. Although, if your traffic is low, this figure can be misleading at first.

How Knowing Your Conversion Rate Can Help You

In sales, small increases in conversion rates can produce profound results. If you have a website generating thousands of views per day, increasing your conversion rate by a percentage point or two will increase your revenue significantly.

By knowing your current conversion rate for a particular product or service, you’ll be able to decipher whether your venture is performing as well as it should be. If it isn’t, changes need to be made for the sake of your balance sheet. You may choose to abandon a particular product range if it’s not selling. You may choose to advertise to a different market. You may even choose to tinker with your website design or your sales copy.  In some instances, when you’re confused as to how to increase your conversion rates, you may want to visit the conversion rate experts.

How Conversion Rate Experts Can Help You

If you need help with conversion rate optimization, it’s nothing to be embarrassed about. Many of the world’s biggest businesses such as Apple, Sony and Microsoft enlist the help of such specialists every year.

Readable Fonts

Whether you’re embarking on a personal web design- or ecommerce web design project, one thing remains constant: You’ll need people to read what it is you write on your site.

One mistake people make is opting for small fonts or those that are unreadable. When choosing yours, remember to consider tablet and mobile users. Also make sure your fonts contrast from your site’s background. Green on green, for example, just won’t work.

Responsive Browsing

While on the subject of mobile users, it’s now vital that you commit to responsive web design. Last year, research showed more people browsed the web on their mobile than they did on their desktop computer. That means, if your site isn’t responsive, you’re essentially alienating 50% of your customers.

Not only that, but you’ll be punished by Google if your site isn’t responsive. As of 2015, expect your search ranking on Google Mobile to fall if you’re still using a static site.

The 5-Second Rule

The attention span of your average visitor is so short they won’t even have looked at your whole home page before clicking away. While that might sound demoralizing, especially when you’re paying money to a web design agency to make you a nice site, that’s just how things are online.

That’s why it’s always recommended you put your best foot forward. Have a great offer? Put it at the very top of your page, don’t leave it right to the end – people are unlikely to ever see it.
